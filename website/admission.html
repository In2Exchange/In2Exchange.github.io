admission and re-admission policies

1) New students and Transferees

K.C.M. Academy Incorporated admits new students on the bases of the results of the entrance examination, personal interview,academic records, and character information. To be eligible, they should also meet the following:

Basic Admission Requirements

a. Pre school

  1. NSO Birth certificate (not hospital certificate)
    2. One (1) Original copy to be presented
    One (1) Photocopy to be submitted
    (DepEd Order No. 47.s. 1998)
  2. Two (2) I.D. Pictures (1x1) with the name of the applicant written at the back

b. Grade One

  1. Comply with Preschool requirements nos. 1 and 2
  2. Preschool Report Card
  3. Pass the K.C.M.A's entrance examination

c. Grade Two to Ten

  1. Current Report Card and Form 137
  2. Conduct Grade not lower then 80% or Certificate of Good Moral Character coming from previous school attended.
  3. NSO Birth certificate (not hospital certificate)
    4. One (1) Original copy to be presented
    One (1) Photocopy to be submitted
    (DepEd Order No. 47.s. 1998)
  4. Two (2) I.D. Picture (1x1) with the name of the applicant written at the back
  5. Pass the K.C.M.A's entrance examination

2) Admission Procedure

  1. Present required documents to the School Office for evaluation
  2. Secure application form and pay testing fee at the School Office
  3. Submit all requirements to the School Office:
    • Application form (duly accomplished)
    • Testing Feel Receipt
    • Two (2) I.D. Pictures (1x1) with the name of the applicant written at the back
    • Birth Certificate (Photocopy)
  4. Take the entrance examination as scheduled. Applicants must be present ten (10) minutes before the testing time.
  5. For applicants who pass the entrance test, proceed to the School Office to pay for reservation fee or pre-enroll for the coming school year.(The reservation fee is deductible upon enrollment but no refundable and non-transferable)

3) Policy on the Re-admission of Old High School Students

  • Student's re-admission to the next school year depends upon their ACADEMIC and CONDUCT performance in the previous school year.
  • Students who fail in conduct in any grading period will be placed under CONDUCT PROBATION and unless the probation status is lifted within the period specified in the agreement he/she may be dismissed from the school during or at the end of the school year.

    • For High School Students only:

    1. All students both old and new under ACADEMIC PROBATION are required to attend Remedial sessions until status is lifted.
    2. Students who fail in one or two final ratings will be placed on ACADEMIC PROBATION the following school year after passing summer extension classes.
    3. Parents of students with failures and disciplinary problems must see the School Head before enrollment.
